What exactly is cyberbullying?

Definition

It is a form of bullying that happens online or through electronic means. Another name for it is online bullying. It takes place in messaging apps, online games, chat rooms, forums, and social media. In cyberbullying, a person stalks, abuses, threatens, and harasses another using technology. Usually, they send rude messages to embarrass and hurt the victim. It also includes posting sensitive information and data about someone to harm them. This form of bullying is a serious threat to everyone, as it can harm one's mental health and well-being. It can damage one's self-esteem and create emotional scars. It has become prevalent, especially because we live in a digital world where technology has advanced rapidly.

Common Tactics and Examples

Cyberbullying includes:

  • Sharing one's personal information online without their consent
  • Posting rude comments about someone
  • Impersonation
  • Sexual harassment
  • Blackmailing
  • Sending hurtful and unpleasant texts
  • Leaking private photos and videos of someone
  • Spreading rumors and gossip online
  • Verbally abusing someone during multiplayer online games
  • Using inappropriate photos to bully others
  • Video shaming
  • Creating a website or blog that aims to humiliate and hurt the victim

This video discusses more information about the common examples and other popular forms of cyberbullying.

Why we should be concerned

We should all be concerned since all forms of bullying can greatly affect a person's life, well-being, self-esteem, and mental health. We should not downplay the seriousness of cyberbullying and its harmful effects on a person. With the internet becoming more accessible to people, cyberbullying has become more common, and more and more people are affected. Children and teenagers are often targets of it, and it can lead to serious consequences on their overall health. No one deserves to be bullied, abused, and harassed.
